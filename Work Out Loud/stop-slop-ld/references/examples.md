# Before/After Examples: Learning Design Edition

## 1. Course narration opener

**Before (slop):**
> Welcome to this module on giving feedback. In today's fast-paced workplace, the ability to deliver effective feedback has never been more important. By the end of this lesson, you will be able to understand the key principles of constructive feedback and apply them in a variety of workplace situations. Let's get started!

**After:**
> Most feedback conversations go wrong in the first thirty seconds. Not because managers don't care — because they lead with the rating instead of the observation. This module is about that gap: what to say before you say how it went.

---

## 2. Learning objective

**Before (slop):**
> By the end of this module, learners will be able to:
> - Understand the importance of active listening
> - Appreciate the value of nonverbal communication
> - Gain awareness of common communication barriers in the workplace

**After:**
> After this module, you can diagnose a conversation that went sideways and identify the specific moment it broke down — whether that was an assumption, a missed cue, or a poorly timed question.

---

## 3. Scenario setup

**Before (slop):**
> Meet Alex. Alex is a team lead at a growing tech company. Alex has recently been promoted and is navigating the challenges of managing former peers. In this scenario, Alex must decide how to handle a difficult performance conversation.

**After:**
> You were promoted eight months ago. The person you're about to review was your closest work friend before that. Their numbers are off. You've been avoiding this conversation for three weeks. It's Tuesday. The meeting is in twenty minutes.

---

## 4. Incorrect answer feedback in a scenario

**Before (slop):**
> Not quite! That's an understandable choice, but it might not be the most effective approach in this situation. Think about how the other person might feel, and consider what outcome you're hoping to achieve. Let's look at another option!

## 6. Importance declaration

**Before (slop):**
> This skill is critical in today's workplace. As organizations become more complex and teams more distributed, the ability to communicate clearly across functions has significant implications for both individual performance and organizational success.

**After:**
> Cross-functional miscommunication is the most common reason projects slip. Not technical problems — someone assumed someone else had it.

---

## 7. Summary / wrap-up

**Before (slop):**
> In this module, we explored the key principles of inclusive design. We learned about the importance of accessibility, discovered strategies for creating more inclusive content, and gained awareness of common barriers that users with disabilities face. As you continue your learning journey, remember to apply these principles in your day-to-day work. You've got this!

**After:**
> Accessibility isn't a checklist at the end of a project. It's a constraint you design toward from the start. The three things that break most learning content for screen reader users: unlabeled images, PDF-heavy delivery, and videos with no captions. Start there.

---

## 8. Job aid / reference card intro

**Before (slop):**
> This job aid is designed to provide you with a quick reference guide to support you in navigating the performance review process. It contains key steps and helpful tips to ensure a smooth and effective experience for both you and your team members.

**After:**
> Use this when you're in the middle of a review cycle and can't remember the sequence. It won't replace the training — it's the cheat sheet for when you're already in the conversation.
